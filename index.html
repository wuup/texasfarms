The Story Behind Our Name

Why "Texas" in Vermont?

Our farm sits on historic agricultural land that was once a thriving Vermont homestead. The name "Texas Farms" has a charming connection to local history:

"A local lad, caught up in the westward migration frenzy, vowed he was leaving and would never again have to use a side-hill plow. Instead, he settled and farmed near Concord Corners on his ancestral ridges. His amused neighbors playfully dubbed the area 'Texas'."

Today, we're honored to breathe new life into this cherished piece of Vermont farmland, restoring its agricultural heritage while building toward a sustainable future. There's something magical about returning this land to its original purpose - growing food and nurturing life, just as generations before us did.

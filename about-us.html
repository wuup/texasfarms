About Us

Our journey from dream to reality

The Beginning

This farm has been a dream long in the making. In rural Norfolk, England, Alan grew up playing (and causing some mischief) in nearby farmers' fields. Over the years, his love for the countryside morphed into a yearning to farm. His dreams of farming in the U.K. were difficult to realize with the high prices of limited land. After moving to the U.S. for work in 2012, he figured his dream would need to wait till retirement to be realized.

Heather grew up in the suburbs of Massachusetts but had very rarely put her hand to soil. Despite this, she had always dreamt of having a home with lots of land, animals, and gardens. Throughout our courtship, we spoke about our dreams and originally planned to pursue them as a later-in-life goal. During the 2020 pandemic, while it felt like the world was burning around us it made us truly think about the kind of life we want to lead and what would fulfill us most. Why wait till retirement to make our dreams come true?

Finding Our Home

In 2021, we purchased an abandoned off-grid logging property in the highlands of the Northeast Kingdom of Vermont. Fully wooded, we had little idea of what our new property had to offer. After clearing around 4 acres of regen growth in the fall of 2022, we discovered an old apple orchard and several old stone walls. Upon further research, this land was once part of an old farm back in the 1800s and the area is known locally as Texas.

Why Texas?

William Alexander with the Vermonter tells the tale best:

"The story goes that a local lad, caught up in the westward migration frenzy, vowed he was leaving and would never again have to use a side-hill plow. Instead, he settled and farmed near Concord Corners on his ancestral ridges and his neighbors twitted him by calling the area Texas."

If you search for 'Texas Vermont', today on Google Maps it will direct you to our farm.

Our Farm Today

We are a small off-grid, no till farm that focuses on raising heritage breed animals and growing uncommon heirloom varieties of vegetables and fruit. Our farmstand is always open and accepts cash only.

Texas Farms: A Story of Legacy

Where the spirit of the gold rush meets off-grid, regenerative farming.

Why "Texas" in Vermont?

Our farm sits on old farmland that a young lad once called home. He was caught up in the westward migration frenzy of the gold rush and was desperate to join in. He vowed that he was going to leave for Texas and would never have to use his side-plow ever again. In fact, he told so many people in town that when he eventually stayed and settled at his ancestral home, the townsfolk declared the area Texas. Though he didn't make it to Texas (not in Vermont), his farming legacy lives on in us.

From Untouched Forest to Thriving Farm

Over the past four years, we've built our off-grid, regenerative farm from the ground up on a wooded mountain. No electricity, no roads—just the determination to raise real food in harmony with the land.

Our Commitment to Quality

At a time when much of our food is mass-produced and questionable, we're dedicated to doing things right:

Heritage Breed Livestock

Heritage breed livestock are an irreplaceable piece of earth's biodiversity, offering incredible variety that may be needed for future farms – robust health, mothering instincts, foraging, and the ability to thrive in a changing climate. These farm animals are a vital part of ensuring food security for our planet – now and for the future!

Open-Pollinated + Heirloom Produce

The vast majority of the produce we grow here in Texas are open-pollinated and heirloom varieties. These varieties compose an irreplaceable genetic resource that contributes to the security of our future food supply.

Regenerative Methods

We practice no-till farming, meaning the soil is disturbed as little as possible. This allows us to limit soil erosion, capture carbon in the soil, use less amendments and fertilizers, limit the use of large farming equipment, and foster the health of the microbiome in the soil.

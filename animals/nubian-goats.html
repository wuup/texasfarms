Nubian Goats

The Queens of Milk Production

About the Breed

Our purebred ADGA registered Nubian goats are known for their distinctive floppy ears and exceptional milk production.

We raise purebred ADGA registered Nubian goats (the ones with the floppy ears). They are known for their rich, sweet tasting milk which is perfect for cheesemaking. We focus on correct confirmation and high milk yield. All of our goats are well socialized and easy to handle. We are a CAE-free tested herd. Our herd was added to the farm in 2023 and we're expecting our first babies in 2025.

Breed Characteristics

Superior Milk Production

Known for rich, high-butterfat milk perfect for cheese and soap making.

Friendly Temperament

Social and affectionate nature makes them excellent family farm animals.

Climate Adaptable

Thrive in Vermont's varied climate conditions throughout the year.

Life on Our Farm

Daily Life

Our Nubian goats enjoy spacious pastures where they can browse on a variety of vegetation. They have access to clean, comfortable barns and plenty of climbing structures to satisfy their natural instincts.

Milking Program (coming in 2026)

We will maintain a careful milking schedule in 2026, with does being milked twice daily. Their milk is used for our artisanal cheese production and natural soap making. All milk will be pasteruized.

Breeding Program

Our breeding program focuses on maintaining the high standards of the Nubian breed while selecting for traits that excel in our Vermont climate.

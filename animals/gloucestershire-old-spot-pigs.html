Gloucestershire Old Spot Pigs

The Orchard Pig of England

About the Breed

The oldest pedigreed spotted pig in the world, favored by British Royalty for its exceptional meat quality.

The Gloucestershire Old Spot pig is the oldest pedigreed spotted pig in the world. Once popular on small family farms where they were raised on dropped orchard fruit earning them the nicknames of the "orchard pig" or "cottage pig". This breed is known for being excellent foragers, attentive mothers, and their gentle temperaments.

The richly marbled meat has a depth of flavor that is completely unmatched. It's no surprise this breed is the favorite of the British Royalty!

Breed Characteristics

Excellent Foragers

Natural grazers that thrive on pasture, reducing feed costs and maintaining the land.

Gentle Temperament

Known for their docile nature and excellent maternal instincts.

Superior Meat Quality

Produces well-marbled, flavorful meat perfect for artisanal pork products.

Life on Our Farm

Daily Life

Our Gloucestershire Old Spots spend their days roaming our pastures and woodlands, foraging for roots, grass, and fallen fruit. They have access to comfortable shelters and mud wallows for natural cooling and skin protection.

Breeding Program

We maintain a careful breeding program to preserve the pure genetics of this rare breed while selecting for traits that thrive in Vermont's climate. Our sows typically raise two litters per year.

Sustainable Practices

These pigs play a crucial role in our farm's ecosystem, helping to clear land, fertilize soil, and reduce food waste by consuming excess produce and windfall fruit.

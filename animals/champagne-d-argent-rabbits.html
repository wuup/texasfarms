Champagne D'Argent Rabbits

The Silver Heritage Breed

About the Breed

The Champagne D'Argent is one of the oldest rabbit breeds, known for its unique silvering coat and excellent meat quality.

The Champagne D'Argent rabbit is a French heritage breed prized for its meat and pelts. It was first recorded in France during the 1600s making it one of the oldest meat rabbit breeds. Not only do they have calm temperaments, good mothering instincts, and fast growth rates, they also change color. Champagne D'Argent kits are born completely black, gradually turning silver as they grow. Silver hairs begin to show around 4 weeks and are fully silver by 5 to 6 months.

Breed Characteristics

Unique Coloring

Distinctive silvering process that creates a beautiful champagne-silver coat.

Gentle Nature

Calm and docile temperament makes them excellent for handling and showing.

Sustainable Choice

Efficient feed conversion and good mothering abilities for sustainable farming.

Life on Our Farm

Daily Life

Our Champagne D'Argent rabbits enjoy spacious, clean hutches with plenty of enrichment activities. They receive a balanced diet of quality pellets, fresh hay, and seasonal vegetables.

Breeding Program

We maintain careful breeding records and select for healthy, productive animals with excellent temperaments and proper color development.

Sustainable Practices

Our rabbits contribute to our farm's sustainability through efficient feed conversion and providing valuable fertilizer for our gardens.
